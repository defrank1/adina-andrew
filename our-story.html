Our Story

Nobody can remember when exactly we first met. We both agree it was sometime in Fall 2019, when our respective DC friend groups started overlapping at each other's parties in Columbia Heights. Sometime in February 2020, we all made plans to start a kickball team that spring, which gave Andrew an opportunity to add Adina as a friend on the only social media platform of hers he could find — Facebook. But then, the pandemic hit.

About a year and a half later, we matched on a dating app. Adina greeted Andrew with the nickname she knew him by from her friends — "Transit Andy!" Our first date was far from picturesque. It was a cold, rainy night in the makeshift streatery outside of Hank's Oyster Bar in Dupont, in the middle of the Omicron wave.

After the second date, Andrew knew Adina was special. But it took Adina until the third date — cooking dinner at Andrew's — for the feeling to become mutual.

Over the next few months, we fell in love, while both applying to grad schools and not knowing what the future would hold.

We met each other's families.

Toward the start of summer, we had a long, hard conversation. Andrew was moving to New York for grad school, and Adina was staying in town to go to Georgetown Law. Through tears, we agreed to give long distance a shot.

At Andrew's goodbye party before moving to New York, in Malcolm X Park

It's hard to believe we spent the next two years long-distance, both in school, making frequent trips up and down the Northeast Corridor to see each other almost every other weekend. Those formative years made the heart grow fonder, sure, but they also taught us patience, commitment, and appreciation for each other.

Since we moved in together in 2024, we've loved building a shared life in DC, and are so lucky to have visited so many amazing places.

When Andrew started thinking of where to propose to Adina last year, none of the monuments downtown felt right. And Adina was expecting him to hide his proposal plan very poorly. So, Andrew had to pick out an unassuming spot. He chose the place where that teary-eyed conversation about going long-distance took place three years earlier. Adina thought they were meeting friends for drinks just a couple blocks further down the street. Like she always does, she asked Andrew "Remember when we almost broke up here?!" But this time was different.
